Terry Crews

Terry Crews is a 47 year old engineering and astrophysics professor at Michigan State University, where he has been teaching and doing his own projects and research for the last 20 years.

Facts

Terry currently is the head of the head of the Engineering department at Michigan State, and has been awarded the Engineer of the Year award for the state of Michigan for the past 10 years in a row. He has helped design many potential spaceships that could reach different galaxies at speeds higher that light speed if advances are made with harvesting energy.

Interests and Values

Terry has devoted his life to Michigan State, to the point where it seems like he never leaves. He loves working with colleagues and students with their different projects. He also loves watching documentaries in his free time.

Computer, Internet, and TV Use

Terry currently owns a Macbook Pro laptop, an iPhone 8, an iPad, and a Google Watch. He uses these for work, research, emailing colleagues and students, watching documentaries, etc.

A Typical Day

  • Terry gets up at 7 am every day, and gets to his office by 8 am in case there are students who need assistance.On Tuesday's and Thursday's, he teaches an 8 am Astronomy 101 course.
  • At 10am, he meets with his colleagues and students whom he is working on projects with to go over any updates or important information.
  • at 12:40 Monday through Friday, he also teaches; Monday and Wednesday it's ENG 430, Tuesday and Thursday is AST 400, and Friday it's ISP 205 Lab.
  • at 3 pm, he works on his individual projects for about three hours.
  • He gets dinner on campus around 6 pm, and heads home at about 7:30 pm.
  • When he gets home, he watches a documentary and then goes to bed at midnight.

Future Goals

Terry hopes that his ships make it into space in the near future, and that interstellar travel becomes a norm. He would also love to keep teaching at Michigan State as long as he can.

Quote

"I love what I do and where I am in my life."
